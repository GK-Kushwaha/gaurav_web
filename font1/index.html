Dr. Norman Borlaug

The man who saved a billion lives

Here's a time line of Dr.Borlaug's life:

  • ::marker 1914 "-Born in Cresco, Iowa"
  • ::maker 1933 "- Leaves his family's farm to attend the University of Minnesota, thanks to a Depresssion era program known as the "National Youth Administration" "
  • ::marker 1935 "- Has to stop school and save up more money. Works in the Civilian Conservation Crops, hepling starving Americans."I saw food changed them", he said. "All of this left scars on me." "
  • ::maker 1937 "- Finishes university and takes a job in the US Forestry service"
  • ::maker 1938 "- Marries wife of 69 years Margret Gibson. Gets laid off due to bugets cuts. Inspired by Elvin charles Stakman, he returns to school study under Stakman, who teaches him about breeding pest-resisdent plants."
  • ::maker 1941 "- Tries to enroll in the military after the Pearl Harbor attack, but is rejected. instead, the military asked his lab to work on waterproof glue, DDT to control malaria, disinfectants, and other applied science."
  • ::maker 1942 "- Receives a Ph.d. in Genetics and Plant Pathology "
  • ::maker 1944 "- Rejects a 100% salary increase from Doupont, leaves behind his pregnant wifi, and flies to Mexico to head a new plant pathology program. over the next 16 years, his team breeds 6,000 different strains of disease resistent wheat - including different varieties for each major climate on Earth."
  • ::maker 1945 "- Discovers a way to grown wheat twice each season, doubling wheat yields"
  • ::maker 1953 "- crosses a short, sturdy dwarf breed with a high-yeilding American breed, creating a strin that responds well to fertilizer. It goes on to provide 95% of Mexico's wheat."
  • ::maker 1962 "- Visits Delhi and brings his high-yielding strains of wheat to the indian subcontinent in time to help mitigate mass starvation due to a rapidly expanding population"
  • ::maker 1970 "- receives the Nobel Peace Prize"
  • ::maker 1983 "- helps seven African countries dramatically increase their maize and sorghum yields"
  • ::maker 1984 "- become a distinugushed professor at texas A&M university"
  • ::maker 2005 "- states "we will have to double the world food supply by 2050." Argues that genetically modified crops are the only way we can meet the demand, as we run out of arable land. Says that GM crops are not inherently dangerous because for a long time. Long before we called it science, people were selecting the best breeds. "
  • ::maker 2009 "- dies at the age of 95."
